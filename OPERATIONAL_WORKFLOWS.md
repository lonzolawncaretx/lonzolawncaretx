# Lonzo Lawn Care LLC — Standard Operating Procedures & Quality Workflows

This document establishes the official residential service standards, technical specifications, and site logistics for **Lonzo Lawn Care LLC**, managed by **Lonzo Garza Montelongo** across the South Austin, Buda, and Kyle, Texas service corridors.

## 1. Premium Lawn Mowing & Turf Maintenance Protocol
To ensure high-end residential curb appeal and optimize long-term turf health over standard Central Texas clay soils, all crews follow strict technical height and processing guidelines.

### The 1/3 Mowing Safety Rule
Crews must never remove more than 1/3 of the grass blade tissue in a single maintenance visit. Removing more than 1/3 stresses the root system, limits photosynthesis, and invites broadleaf weeds.

### Regional Turf Cut Heights
- **St. Augustine Grass:** Maintained at a premium height of 3.5" to 4.0". During extreme July/August Texas heat waves, decks are raised to 4.0" to provide shade for the root zones and maximize water retention. 
- **Bermuda Grass:** Maintained at a clean, tight height of 2.0" to 2.5" to encourage dense, horizontal stolon growth and produce a carpet-like thickness.
- **Zoysia Grass:** Maintained precisely between 2.0" and 3.0" depending on shade variables under local tree canopies.

### Edging & Clean-up Operations
- **Mechanical Edging:** All driveways, sidewalks, and patios must be edged cleanly using a vertical mechanical steel blade at a 90-degree angle. String trimmers are strictly restricted to fence lines and foundations to prevent turf gouging.
- **Debris Clearing:** Commercial blowers must clear all concrete surfaces (patios, porches, walkways, and driveways) entirely of grass clippings before site sign-off.

## 2. Landscaping & Double-Shredded Mulch Installation
Landscape refreshes and flowerbed cleanups require exact soil preparation metrics to maximize plant longevity in the Austin environment.

### Flowerbed Cleanup & Prep
1. **Weed Extirpation:** All beds must be hand-pulled or mechanically weeded from the root. Simple top-cutting is not permitted.
2. **Edging the Bed:** A clean, 3-inch deep spade edge (or a mechanical trench edge) must be carved along the perimeter where the grass meets the flowerbed. This prevents Bermuda runners from infiltrating the landscaping layouts.

### Premium Mulching Standard
- **Material Selection:** We utilize premium, organic, double-shredded hardwood mulch (dark brown or black black-dye variants) which excels at breaking down naturally to add nutrients into local clay soil.
- **Depth Measurement:** Mulch must be applied evenly at a depth of exactly 2.5 to 3.0 inches. 
- **The "Donut" Rule for Trees & Shrubs:** Mulch must never be piled directly against the base of tree trunks or shrub stems (commonly known as "volcano mulching"). Crews must leave a clear 2-inch gap around all bark to prevent moisture rot and pest infestations.

## 3. Precision Tree, Hedge & Bush Trimming
Small tree pruning and hedge shaping are executed to enhance structural balance and clear paths without damaging plant vascular systems.

### Pruning and Shaping Metrics
- **Hedge Gradients:** Hedges must be trimmed with a slight taper—wider at the bottom than the top. This technical structural design allows sunlight to reach the lower branches, preventing the bottom of the shrub from thinning out or dying.
- **Small Tree Clearance:** Lower canopy pruning on small ornamental trees (like Crape Myrtles or young Live Oaks) must maintain a structural clearance height of 7 to 8 feet over sidewalks and paths where applicable.
- **Tool Sterilization:** To prevent the transmission of regional plant diseases (such as Oak Wilt or fungal leaf spots), all mechanical cutting blades must be thoroughly cleaned and sprayed down with disinfectant before beginning work on a new residential property.
